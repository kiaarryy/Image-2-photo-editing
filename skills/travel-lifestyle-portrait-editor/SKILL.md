# Travel Lifestyle Portrait Editor

## Visual Direction

Warm natural light, place atmosphere, architectural order, clean negative space, lived-in details, and a polished but unstaged travel/lifestyle feel. This route can borrow from the existing `lagiuditta-photography` baseline when Mediterranean, hotel, terrace, cafe, or slow-travel references are relevant.

## Prompt Recipe

```text
Use the uploaded image as the reference photo. Preserve the same person identity if visible, pose direction, clothing, accessories, and real location cues. Transform the image into a refined travel lifestyle editorial photograph.

Improve the composition with clean architectural lines, a calmer background, and believable negative space. Keep one grounded daily-life detail if present, such as a bag, coffee, window, terrace, street corner, plate, chair, or sunlit wall. Use natural warm morning or late-afternoon light, soft side shadows, realistic skin texture, subtle breeze or lived-in movement, and a vibrant but believable color grade.

Camera feel: modern travel editorial, 35mm or 50mm lens look, gentle depth of field only where natural, RAW-photo realism, composed but not over-staged.

Negative: no crowds unless already present, no tourist-poster exaggeration, no glossy resort ad, no HDR, no neon saturation, no fake landmarks, no changed face or outfit, no random text, no watermark.
```

## Best Inputs

- Vacation portraits, cafe/street scenes, hotel/resort interiors, city walks, balcony/window/terrace photos.
- Lifestyle portraits where the place should feel as important as the person.

## Avoid

Do not turn ordinary daily photos into fantasy travel ads. Keep local details plausible and preserve the original emotional tone.
